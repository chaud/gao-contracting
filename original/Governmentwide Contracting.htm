A Snapshot: Government-Wide Contracting

A 2025 Update

The federal government obligated $793 billion through contracts in fiscal year 2025, a $17.8 billion increase from fiscal year 2024 after adjusting for inflation.

Who got the contracts?

How did they buy?
The overall competition rate in fiscal year 2025 was 66%, the same as fiscal year 2024. Civilian agencies' competition rate was 89% and Defense agencies about 52%.

What is the trend in spending through Other Transaction Agreements?

Other transaction agreements offer agencies a flexible way to purchase goods and services. In fiscal year 2025, agencies reported $18.2 billion in spending on these agreements, a $700 million decrease from fiscal year 2024.

Data on other transaction agreements are not included in data on federal contract obligations.
